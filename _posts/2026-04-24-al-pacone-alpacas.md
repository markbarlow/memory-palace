Not long ago we went to walk the alpacas at [Botany Bay farm](https://www.botanybayfarm.co.uk/alpacaexperiences) on the edge of London. A charming experience with a passionate farmhand and photogenic animals.

This brought back memories of a hostel in La Paz, where the graffiti on the back of a toilet door was exclusively alpaca-themed. Much of the scrawling could have been written about sheep in another location, and followed a common and predictable trope. "Alpacas: wear 'em, eat 'em, fuck 'em - you decide" - and many variations thereof. 

However, there was one which stood out and made me laugh. The uniquely brilliant and simple "Al Pacone". I can visualize its position, off to the upper left towards the edge of the door frame. Easy to miss physically. Easy to miss semantically too, by virtue of [typoglycaemia](https://www.mrc-cbu.cam.ac.uk/people/matt.davis/cmabridge/). Set apart, distinct, and a cut above the rest.
